From Europe to the World

This story will explain how Formula 1 grew from a mostly European championship into a global sport, then connect that expansion to the constructors and drivers who defined each competitive era.

1. The question

Formula 1 began with a compact European calendar. The story starts by asking how that calendar became global and why different eras of dominance emerged.

2. European origins

Show the early circuits first. This scene should make Europe feel like the baseline before the story expands outward.

3. Expansion beyond Europe

Animate new race locations by year or decade. The transition should feel like the sport is spreading from a regional calendar into a global one.

4. The calendar becomes global

Change from individual circuit points to an aggregated continent timeline. This is the bridge between geography and competition.

5. Constructor eras

Keep the same time axis, but shift attention from where F1 raced to which teams controlled points and championships during each era.

6. Drivers inside dominant teams

Drill into selected constructor eras to show the drivers behind the dominance. This keeps driver analysis connected to the main story instead of feeling separate.

7. Explore the full history

End with open interaction: scrub years, select constructors, and compare driver eras after the guided story has taught the audience how to read the views.
